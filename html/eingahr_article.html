Eingahr is a metal alloy with similar composition to cupronickel. It is used to construct devices for manipulating ambient energy due to its resistance to corrosion from Draihar oil compounds and ambient insulating properties.

Material: Metal Alloy

Composition

Eingahr is a metal alloy typically composed of majority copper, with the addition of nickel and zinc. Throughout the forging process, ambient energy is directed into the mass of molten metal.

Alternative Compositions

Alternative compositions have been tested with varying results. It has been reported that a higher percentage of nickel content can increase resistance to corrosion, at the cost of reduced maleability.

The properties and qualities of Eingahr can vary slightly depending on where it was forged and where the materials were sources. It is speculated that variations in the ambient field at different locations can affect the outcome of the forging process.

Properties

Eingahr has been observed to be resistant to the corrosive properties of Draihar oil and its derivatives, with high quality alloys maintaining their integrity even after decades of regular contact. Additionally, it is capable of heavily reducing the leakage of ambient energy from energized materials (such as Draihar oil) back into the ambient field. For these reasons it is a popular material of choice for many standard mystical tools and storage devices, as well as machinery involved in processing Draihar oil. However, its insulating property can inhibit channeling of ambient energy from the substances contained within, so some designs may opt from more alternative materials.
