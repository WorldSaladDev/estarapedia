Dreiczel is a human city, and the administrative capital of Tir Moran. It is the third most populous city in Tir Moran, with approximately 56,000 residents.

Geography

Dreiczel is located where the Eyrfeyllf river feeds into the Dreic sea. The surrounding plains are temperate, hilly, and sparsely wooded

History

Dreiczel was almost entirely destroyed near the end of the 51Y.E. Temchael civil war in order to eliminate resources that would have been gained by the Tir Moran resistance. It has since been partially rebuilt in the same location due to the favorable geography, and has become the capital city of Tir Moran. Since the war, rebuilding efforts have scavenged what remained of the old city in an effort to recover expensive materials like stone for new construction. As of 100Y.E. most of the city center has been rebuilt, but thousands of structures outside the city walls remain in ruin. These ruins have been designated Lothzel (lit. "past city") district.

Demographics

As of the census in 100 Y.E. Dreiczel had a permanent population of approximately 56,000. Of the population, about 99% are human (of which 81% are Temric, 14% are Hamshi, and 6% are others), and 1% are nirrall. The age demographics of Dreiczel were: 30% under the age of 14, 25% between the ages of 15-24, 35% between the ages of 25-49, and 10% over the age of 50.

About 60% of working age citizens (between the age of 15-49) were independently employed, primarily in agriculture, then as craftsmen and mercenaries. 15% were employed under guilds. 10% were employed by the government. 15% were unemployed or underemployed.

Median income was above the national average.

Districts

Dreiczel has nine administrative districts.

King's District

The King's District (Temric: Adeytyairllan'un Eynzel) consists almost entirely of large manors of wealthy nobles and merchants. The King's District also features the King's Harbor, where the piers are privately owned.

Harbor District

The harbor district (Temric: Aindey Eynzel) is the public harbor district. A number of small merchant stalls can also be found operating here during the day.

People's District

The People's District (Temric: Ithngv큰r'un Eynzel) is the primary middle-income residential district within the walls of Dreiczel. The residents consist mostly of merchants and artizans.

Trading District

The Trading District (Temric: Maisait'deyn Eynzel) is the market district. The Merchant's Gate (Temric: Maisait'ur Koeynsh) here allows access into the city. All gates into Dreiczel are guarded, and only open during the day.

President's District

The President's District (Temric: Eydeynv큰r'un Eynzel) is the district surrounding the Eydeynv큰r's castle. Many government facilities are located in this district, along with businesses targetting visiting diplomats and businessmen, such as upscale inns, brothels and wineries.

Agricultural land along the Eyrfeyllf river falls under Eydeynv큰r'un Eynzel jurisdiction.

Northern Gate

The Northern Gate District (Temric: Ngu'yir Koeynsh Eynzel) surrounds the northern gate into the walls of Dreiczel. It is a mixed purpose, primarily low-income district, with slums outside the walls.

Eastern Gate

The Northern Gate District (Temric: Naiel'yir Koeynsh Eynzel) surrounds the two eastern gates into the walls of Dreiczel. It is a mixed purpose, primarily low-income district, with slums outside the walls.

Lothzel

Lothzel is the designation for the ruins that remain from the 51Y.E. Temchael civil war. Parts of the district near the walls are inhabited, but most of the district is undeveloped ruins.

Western Gate

The Northern Gate District (Temric: Staer'yir Koeynsh Eynzel) surrounds the wastern gate into the walls of Dreiczel. The district is a mix of low and medium income housing and entertainment.
