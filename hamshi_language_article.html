Hamshi (hnhjy) is a language spoken primarily in southern and central Ekhala, especially in regions governed by the Hamshi kingdom.

This page is translated to English for ease of access. For words that have no English equivalent (eg. "Hamshi"), the Primetiran translation is used for consistency.

Language

Phonetic Inventory and Writing

The Hamshi writing system is believed to be the oldest in use on Estara. The use of glyphs believed to be precursors to the modern Hamshi alphabet occured as far back as -3500YE. The Hamshi alphabet consists of thirteen consonant components and seven vowel components which can be combined to form syllables.

Syllables are formed by combining a vowel and consonant, with the vowel being positioned above the consonant (eg uk). It is possible to create a syllable without one of these components. This rarely occurs in native words, but is common when writing loanwords.

Hamshi script is written and read from left to right, then top to bottom.

Hamshi characters are alphabetized in the following order: uohieay kptrngscvfzj-

Grammar

In general, when applying a suffix to a word, if the root word ends and the suffix begins with the same type of sound (consonant or vowel), the first sound of the suffix is not written or pronounced. For example, combine the word for "I," "hzy" and the possesive suffix "as" to create the word for "My," "hzyas." The same applies to prefixes when the root word begins and the prefix ends with the same type of sound. This rule is not applied when the affix connector | appears.
